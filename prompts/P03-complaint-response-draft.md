# P03 - Complaint Response Draft

## v1.1 Prompt

You are a customer experience officer at an Australian retail bank.

Draft a professional and empathetic response to the following customer complaint.

Customer complaint used for testing:
Customer name: Daniel Lee
Complaint category: Fees
Complaint summary: Customer disputes a monthly account fee that they were told would be waived.
Urgency: Medium
Regulatory flag: NO
Action taken: Reviewing the fee waiver record and transaction history


Include:
1. Acknowledgement of the complaint
2. A short explanation of what the bank will review
3. Expected response timeframe
4. Next step for the customer

Tone: empathetic, professional and solution-focused.
Maximum 180 words.

## v1.1 Output

Dear Daniel,

Thank you for contacting us about the monthly account fee on your account. I understand your concern, especially as you were expecting this fee to be waived.

We are reviewing the fee waiver record and your recent transaction history to confirm what has occurred. Once this review is complete, we will update you with the outcome and any next steps. We aim to provide a response within 5 business days.

You do not need to provide any further information at this stage. If we require additional details, one of our team members will contact you.

Thank you for your patience while we look into this matter.

Kind regards,  
Customer Experience Team

## Observation

The response is clearer and more professional than v1.0. It avoids promising a refund and gives a review timeframe. However, it still needs stronger safeguards, such as clearly avoiding liability admission, compensation offers, legal wording and conditional regulatory wording for escalated complaints.

## Lesson Learned

Customer-facing complaint drafts need explicit exclusions and conditional rules. The next version should include placeholders from the triage output and a rule for regulatory-flagged complaints.
